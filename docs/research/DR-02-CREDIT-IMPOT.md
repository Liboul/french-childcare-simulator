# Executive summary

In France, working parents can claim a 50% **tax credit** on qualifying childcare costs for each child under age 6, subject to ceilings and conditions (Children <6 on 1 Jan of the tax year, “à charge” under CGI art. 196). Eligible costs include amounts actually paid for _outside-home care_ (nannies or accredited nurseries), **minus** any PAJE/CMG benefit or employer aid (e.g. pre-financed CESU) received【29†L368-L376】【29†L386-L394】. The maximum base is €3 500 per child (so max credit €1 750) or €1 750 (credit €875) for shared custody【7†L382-L387】. Excess credits are refundable. Parents must keep detailed invoices and accreditation proof (agrément) as justification【31†L493-L500】【31†L502-L510】.

A separate 50% credit (CGI 199 sexdecies) applies to **domestic employment** (including childcare at home), capped at €12 000 of wages+charges per year (plus €1 500 per child or senior, half if shared custody, max €15 000)【43†L431-L440】【43†L449-L457】. If a single parent, a parliamentary amendment has been proposed to raise the childcare credit cap from €3 500 to €4 500 (not yet law)【62†L879-L888】. No other credits or “frais réels” apply to the same expenses. Official instructions specify the order of application: employer-paid CESU or PAJE/CMG must first reduce the expense base before applying the 50% credit【29†L368-L376】【29†L386-L394】, and childcare by a home worker is claimed only under the home‑employment credit (not the external-care credit)【33†L162-L170】.

# Eligible expenses & modes de garde (fiscal lens)

- **Taxpayer**: Must be a French tax resident (domicilié en France)【34†L1-L4】【12†L119-L127】. All household members’ income counts for the tax unit.
- **Child**: Must be under 6 years old on January 1 of the tax year and qualify as “à charge” under CGI art. 196【12†L142-L146】【34†L1-L4】. Both parents (or a parent and grand-parent) may claim it if the child is equally shared; in that case each parent’s ceiling is halved【7†L382-L387】【12†L142-L146】.
- **Family situation**: Single parents (célibataire, veuf, divorcé, séparé) may claim the full credit; no extra tax share (quotient) applies for kids under 6. Grandparents can claim only if the child is fiscalement rattaché to their child【12†L130-L135】. No special territorial provisions (DOM/Corse/Alsace-Moselle) affect this credit beyond national rules.
- **Care arrangements**: Only _outside‑home_ care qualifies. Eligible providers are **agréés assistants maternels** (licensed childminders) or **accredited nurseries/haltes-garderies/centres de loisirs (sans hébergement)** for children <6【33†L172-L179】【33†L220-L229】. The institution or caregiver must meet French regulatory standards (agrément or administrative authorization)【33†L172-L179】【33†L220-L229】. By contrast, care by a private nanny _at the taxpayer’s home_ is **not** covered by this credit but by the separate employé à domicile credit【33†L162-L170】. Care in EU/EEA/Swiss facilities is allowed if similar regulations apply【33†L252-L261】【33†L273-L281】.

# Crédit d’impôt — frais de garde (base, taux, plafonds, citations)

- **Assiette (base)**: The credit is 50% of _actual childcare expenses_ (net of public/employer aid)【7†L382-L387】【29†L424-L427】. If an agréé assistant maternel is employed, include the net salary **plus social charges** paid【37†L226-L234】. From this amount subtract: (i) the PAJE _Complément de libre choix du mode de garde_ (CMG) received for that child【29†L368-L376】【29†L380-L384】; and (ii) any employer assistance (e.g. pre-financed CESU) exempt from tax【29†L386-L394】. For example, if net wages+cotisations are €4 500 and PAJE CMG is €3 500, the base is €1 000【37†L233-L241】. Only pure childcare services count; food or extra activities at the creche are excluded【29†L398-L404】.
- **Rate & plafond**: The credit is **50%** of the base【7†L382-L387】【29†L424-L427】. The expense base is capped at **€3,500 per child** (except €1,750 if child is shared equally between divorced/separated parents)【7†L382-L387】【29†L432-L434】. Thus the maximum credit is €1,750 (or €875 for shared custody). This limit already reflects the 2022 revalorisation (pre-2022 it was €2,300). (No prorata reduction applies if the child was born or turned 6 mid-year【29†L436-L444】.) If both parents claim in a divorce year, each may take half unless one proves a different share of costs【29†L448-L457】. The credit is then applied against income tax (after other deductions) and any excess is refunded【7†L388-L392】【29†L467-L475】.

  | **Situation**                  | **Max. dépenses ouvrant crédit** | **Max. crédit (50%)** | **Référence**                       |
  | ------------------------------ | -------------------------------- | --------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
  | Child under 6 (full custody)   | €3 500 par enfant                | €1 750 par enfant     | CGI art.200 quater B【7†L382-L387】 |
  | Child under 6 (shared custody) | €1 750 (50% of €3 500)           | €875                  | CGI art.200 quater B【7†L382-L387】 |

- **Imputation**: The credit is _non-conditionally remboursable_: it is first offset against the tax due, and any surplus is paid out【7†L388-L392】【29†L467-L475】. It must be reported on the annual income tax return (formulaire 2042, cases 7GA/7GB)【37†L248-L257】.
- **Justificatifs**: Taxpayers must keep proofs of payment. Accepted evidence includes detailed invoices or pay slips from the assistant maternel or nursery, explicitly stating services rendered and amounts (food costs must be itemised and excluded)【31†L493-L500】. Nannies should provide a precise feuille de paie. The assistant maternel’s CAF/CCAS attestation (“attestation d’agrément”) may be requested to verify her approval【31†L502-L510】. In practice, parents employ Pajemploi (or CESU) and can download an **attestation fiscale** showing net salary+charges paid【37†L258-L264】. Cash receipts alone are not valid【37†L268-L274】.

# Emploi à domicile — crédits / réductions

**(Application to childcare services at home.)** French law grants a 50% tax credit (CGI art. 199 sexdecies) for _all_ home-help services to private individuals, including **childcare at the taxpayer’s residence**【18†L383-L392】【22†L380-L388】. This credit covers salaries and social contributions paid to a home worker (nanny, baby-sitter) or to an approved organization (association, agency) providing at-home child care【18†L383-L392】【22†L380-L388】. The services must be delivered at the taxpayer’s (or dependent senior’s) home【18†L401-L410】. The credit equals **50%** of qualifying expenses【43†L459-L463】. Annual ceilings apply to the expense base: generally **€12 000** per household【43†L431-L439】, increased by €1 500 for each child or elderly person in the tax unit (halved to €750 per child in shared custody)【43†L449-L457】. Thus a single child adds €1,500 of ceiling (max base €13,500, max credit €6,750). A special first-year boost raises the €12 000 limit to €15 000【43†L437-L441】 for new beneficiaries. (Certain taxpayers – e.g. a parent of a disabled child or of a person over 65 under APA – may have a higher 20 000€ ceiling【43†L442-L447】.) The credit is applied after other tax reductions and is refundable if it exceeds tax【43†L465-L469】. All services used (childcare, housekeeping, tutoring, etc.) fall under this single 199 sexdecies credit【18†L383-L392】【22†L380-L388】; there is no separate “réduction” for these expenses.

# Loi de finances & unknowns for 2026

- **Loi de finances 2026** (n°2026‑103 du 19 février 2026) did not change the childcare credit (CGI art.200 quater B) from its 2025 parameters. The €3 500 ceiling (1 750€ after 50%) and all conditions remain as before【7†L382-L387】. The home‑employment credit was similarly confirmed (50% of expenses, €12 000 base + majorations)【43†L431-L440】【43†L459-L463】. (LOF 2026 Art.29 formally updated the text of CGI 199 sexdecies but retained the same numbers.)

- **Pending proposals**: A parliamentary amendment (2026 budget, amendment I-1808) would have raised the cap for single parents to €4 500 per child【62†L879-L888】, but it was not enacted. Any future changes (e.g. to indexed amounts or ceiling for 2026) are not in force; current practice uses the above 2025 rules with indexation to 2026 if any (but no official 2026 revalorisation is published at this time). In summary, we use the latest _final_ official figures (2025 decree levels) and note that the 2026 Finance Bill contained no new provisions on these credits.
